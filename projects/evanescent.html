Combat System

In Evanescent, we aimed to craft a highly dynamic and responsive combat system designed to empower the player with unparalleled freedom of movement and strategic depth. Central to our design is a versatile dash mechanic, allowing players to interrupt or "cancel" any ongoing action instantly. This dash can be executed in four directions: forward, backward, left, and right, enhancing mobility and tactical positioning. However, in air the player is limited to a single dash before needing to land again.

Complementing this mobility is a double-jump feature that enables players to gain substantial height, setting up powerful plunging attacks. Upon executing such an attack, the player impacts the ground, dealing area-of-effect damage to all enemies within a specified radius around the landing point.

The combat toolkit includes a versatile sword with two primary attack modes: a swift, light attack triggered by a left mouse click, ideal for quick engagements with moderate damage, and a slower, heavy attack capable of delivering significant damage.

The system also features an impactful Ultimate ability. When available, indicated by an electrified sword, activating the Ultimate slows down time temporarily, allowing precise targeting based on camera orientation. Upon striking the ground, normal time flow resumes, and the character unleashes three powerful energy slashes radiating outward : one directly ahead, one diagonally left, and one diagonally right. This Ultimate attack, however, is restricted to ground use only and carries a cooldown period of 10 seconds, during which the sword loses its electrified state, visually indicating its unavailability.

Grappling System

The grappling system was designed as a versatile traversal and combat mechanic, aiming to enhance both mobility and tactical decision-making. At designated anchor points in the environment, players can engage the grappling hook to quickly reach elevated locations or strategic vantage points. Upon grappling, the player retains their existing momentum, enabling dynamic movement and seamless transitions between actions. This design choice reinforces a sense of fluidity and speed, essential for both exploration and combat responsiveness.

Beyond traversal, the system plays a critical role in combat scenarios. Players can use grapple points to rapidly reposition, evade incoming attacks, or gain a height advantage over enemies. From elevated positions, they can initiate plunging attacks, rewarding aggressive vertical playstyles and adding an extra layer of strategy to encounters.

The system was carefully tuned to feel responsive and impactful, with attention paid to momentum conservation, input timing, and animation blending. Grapple points are intentionally placed to encourage creative movement paths and provide meaningful combat options, without trivializing level design or encounter difficulty.
